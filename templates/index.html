Data Science for Peace

I am a certified data scientist passionate about building data science products that could serve vulnerable populations.

About me

I am interested in analysing and modeling trends that could negatively affect vulnerable populations in developing countries. This include forced displacement due to conflict and climate change.

I was born and raised in rural Ethiopia. As such, the issue of forced displacement is closer to my heart as I lived through witnessing my country making the headlines on civil war and famine since 1984.

My Portfolio

The interplay between conflict, displacement and climate change are my research interests. However, I acknowledge that it is methodologically challenging to come up with a testable hypothesis to study the topic as a whole due to the existance of multi-directional relationship. For simplicity, I am taking a direct apporach to modeling each trends separately and find ways of integrating the models along the way. My current projects adress the following questions:

  • What is the pattern and magnitude of forced displacement?
  • What is the probability of conflict ?
  • What is the role of land-use change as a driver of displacement?
